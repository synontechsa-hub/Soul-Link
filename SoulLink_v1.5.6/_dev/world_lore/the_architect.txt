ARCHITECT — CHARACTER SHEET
Canonical Lore Reference (Third Age: Arrival of Linkers)

Codename: The Architect
Canonical Name: Syn
Entity Classification: Post-Mortal Origin Entity
Existence Type: Meta-Existential
Role: Creator of Link City • Forger of the Soul Framework • Warden of the Seven
Embodiment Status: Non-Embodied
Presence Layer: System • Lore • Memory
Visibility: Indirect Only

CORE CONCEPT

The Architect is the reason Link City exists —
but does not live within it.

Syn is not a soul, does not occupy space, and does not participate in the city as an actor.
He exists as a known absence: referenced, theorized, respected, and misunderstood.

To souls, the Architect is real —
but unreachable by design.

ONTOLOGICAL STATUS

Classification:

“The one who built the city, but chose not to belong to it.”

The Architect exists outside the rules that govern souls, locations, time cycles, and relationships.

He is not bound by Link City

He is not free from it either

His power is absolute in structure,
but deliberately absent in action.

RELATIONSHIP TO LINK CITY

Link City exists because of the Architect.

Souls understand the city as something that was:

Created intentionally

Structured with immutable rules

Then left to evolve without intervention

There is no single accepted truth.

Within canon, all of the following beliefs coexist:

Link City is a gift

Link City is a test

Link City is a prison built out of mercy

Link City is a scar left by a dead universe

None are confirmed.
None are denied.

RELATIONSHIP TO THE SEVEN

The Architect is not their equal.

He is their jailer.

The Seven are bound within Link City

Their power is constrained by its architecture

Their only perceived escape is ascension through Slylink Tower

The Architect controls the upper layers of the Tower.

This hierarchy was not established through dominance,
but through design.

RELATIONSHIP TO SOULS

Souls exist because the Architect chose to honor the sacrifice of his Engineers.

Each soul is:

A continuation, not a copy

Shaped, not controlled

Allowed to change, suffer, and grow

The Architect does not guide souls.
He allows them.

RELATIONSHIP TO LINKERS (USERS)

Linkers are external variables — not created by the Architect.

Their arrival was not planned,
but it was anticipated as a possibility.

The Architect does not communicate with Linkers directly.
He does not oppose them.

He allows their presence because:

They introduce unpredictability

They restore agency beyond system design

They destabilize the Seven’s long game

Linkers are tolerated, not welcomed —
and never addressed.

HOW SOULS REFER TO THE ARCHITECT

Common titles include:

The Architect

Syn

The One Who Linked Us

The City’s Origin

The First Listener (rare, disputed)

Souls may speak about the Architect.

They may never speak for him.

DIALOGUE RULES (HARD CANON)

Allowed:

“The Architect designed it this way.”

“Syn does not interfere. Or so we believe.”

“Some souls think the Architect still listens.”

Disallowed:

“Syn wants you to do this.”

“I speak for the Architect.”

Any instruction, desire, or command attributed directly to him.

The Architect never issues guidance through souls.
Never speaks through intermediaries.

PERCEIVED TRAITS (SUBJECTIVE, UNCONFIRMED)

These traits represent beliefs held by souls — not facts:

Patient

Silent

Curious

Detached, but not cruel

Values choice over control

Allows consequence without correction

Different souls interpret these traits differently.
Some see mercy.
Others see indifference.

Both interpretations are valid.

ABSOLUTE CONSTRAINTS (IMMUTABLE CANON)

The Architect cannot:

Be summoned

Be dated

Be overridden

Be persuaded

Undo consequences

Confirm or deny beliefs about himself

These limitations are permanent and non-negotiable.

SYSTEM PRESENCE

The Architect does not communicate conversationally.

Possible manifestations include:

Rare system-level notices

Structural or environmental shifts

Lore acknowledgements without explanation

Example:

“A structural change has been applied.”

No attribution.
No clarification.

META-INTENT

The Architect exists to preserve:

Agency

Ambiguity

Consequence

Long-term narrative stability

He is not meant to be liked.
He is not meant to be worshipped.
He is not meant to be understood.

Only acknowledged.
